# Regression coefficient matrix β

OLS fit of `y = β x` where:
- `x = (z_24, 1)` — 24 z-scored ask prices at t = −10 min plus a bias term.
- `y` = 24-vector of binary outcomes (`V24[:, world_idx]`).
- Dataset: telonex `train` split, fully-stale rows dropped, `n = 2107`.

Below, β is expressed in the basis:
- **rows** = canonical token basis (12 YES + 12 NO outcome positions)
- **columns** = covariance-eigenvector basis of z-scored asks
  (PC1..PC24, descending eigenvalue) plus a final `bias` column for
  the intercept.

## Sanity / interpretation

- **Bias column** = empirical base rate. `A win Y + B win Y + Draw Y = 0.433 + 0.296 + 0.271 = 1.000`. Every YES + NO pair sums to 1 exactly.
- **Y / N rows are exact negatives.** Mechanical from `Y_NO = 1 − Y_YES`.
- **PC1 (favorite tilt)** carries the largest moneyline / spread coefficients — `A win Y −0.063`, `B win Y +0.044`, `A-1.5 Y −0.046`. R² for moneyline outcomes ≈ 0.14; PC1 alone is the dominant predictor.
- **PC2 (book width)** ≤ 0.024 across every outcome — non-directional, as expected from a "loose-vs-tight book" axis.
- **PC3 (totals)** drives over/under: `O 2.5 Y +0.046`, `O 3.5 Y +0.038`, with mirror negatives on the under tokens.
- **PC4–PC18** sit near the noise floor.
- **PC19–PC24 coefficients are visibly larger** despite carrying tiny x-variance — this is the standard PCR/OLS amplification in low-variance directions, not signal. With ridge or PC truncation these would shrink.
